Importance-Weight Guidance in Block Diffusion — Rigorous Formulation

2026-07-17 · Companion to MASKED_GUIDANCE.pdf (absorbing-kernel instantiation, Eqs. 1–5 therein) · Implementation: models_seq/bd_models.py :: plan_guided / _denoise_block_mask_guided, models_seq/bd_disc.py, train_bd_disc.py, eval_bd_guidance.py

Question addressed. The importance-weight guidance of MASKED_GUIDANCE.pdf is stated for a fixed-length canvas. Block diffusion (BD3-LM style) generates semi-autoregressively: blocks are committed one at a time and length is emergent. Can the method be applied directly? Answer: yes — the block-autoregressive factorization composes exactly with the per-block guided reverse process, provided the discriminator is defined on partial paths. This note gives the derivation, the two lemmas that make it work (prefix-constant cancellation; the bias of a block-marginal discriminator), and the estimator actually implemented.

1. Setup

Vocabulary: road vertices plus END; canvas form x = [dst, v₀=ori, v₁, …]. The BD model factorizes block-autoregressively over blocks of size B,

pθ(x | od) = ∏b pθ(xb | x<b, od), (1)

each block factor realized by absorbing (masked) diffusion with SUBS x̂₀-prediction and first-hitting sampling. q denotes the target (exceptional-scenario) path distribution over the same canvases. We assume, as in the companion note, the same forward corruption for q and pθ within a block, so q(xbt | xb0) = pθ(xbt | xb0).

2. Block-conditional guided reverse step

Fix the committed prefix x<b and the current noised block xbt. The exceptional-target reverse posterior of the current block, conditioned on the prefix, expands exactly as in the fixed-canvas case but with all measures conditioned on x<b:

q(xbt−1 | xbt, x<b) = 𝔼x₀b ∼ pθ(x₀b | xbt, x<b)[ wb(x₀b) · q(xbt−1 | xbt, x₀b) ] / 𝔼[wb], (2)

wb(x₀b) = q(x₀b | x<b) / pθ(x₀b | x<b). (3)

Eq. (2) is the exact conditional analogue of the companion note's Eq. (1); the forward-kernel ratio q(xbt|x₀b)/pθ(xbt|x₀b) = 1 cancels for the same reason (shared absorbing forward). The only new object is the conditional density ratio (3).

2.1 Lemma 1 (prefix-constant cancellation) — why a partial-path discriminator suffices

Write the conditional ratio via joint (partial-path) ratios:

wb(x₀b) = [ q(x<b, x₀b) / pθ(x<b, x₀b) ] · [ pθ(x<b) / q(x<b) ] = C−1 · r(x<b ∥ x₀b), (4)

where r(u) = q(u)/pθ(u) is the ratio of partial-path marginals and C = q(x<b)/pθ(x<b) depends only on the (fixed) prefix — it is constant across candidates. The guided step (2) is self-normalized: substituting N candidates x₀b,(n) ∼ pθ(x₀ | xbt, x<b) (mean-field, §3),

q̂(xbt−1 | ·) = Σn wb(n) q(xbt−1 | xbt, x₀b,(n)) / Σn wb(n), (5)

any candidate-independent factor of wb cancels between numerator and denominator. Hence using r(x<b ∥ x₀b) in place of wb leaves (5) unchanged: a discriminator trained to tell exceptional from reference partial paths, evaluated on [committed prefix ∥ candidate completion], implements the conditional-ratio guidance exactly. By the Bayes-optimality identity, a partial-path discriminator Dφ trained with positives ∼ q-partials and negatives ∼ pref-partials satisfies D/(1−D) → q(u)/pref(u) = r(u) (with pref = pθ; see §4 on the negative class).

Finite-precision caveat. C cancels algebraically, but if the prefix is already strongly typed (C very large/small) the logit sits in the saturated region of D and candidate-level differences are crushed by finite precision. This motivates the bounded-logit head and label smoothing of §5 — saturation control matters even though C is "harmless" in exact arithmetic.

2.2 Lemma 2 (block-marginal discriminator is biased) — why the prefix must be included

Suppose instead the discriminator saw only the B-token block, estimating the marginal ratio rblk(x₀b) = qblk(x₀b)/pblk(x₀b) where qblk(y) = Σu q(u) q(y|u) marginalizes over prefixes u. Then

rblk(y) / wb(y) = [ Σu q(u | y) … ] — a function of y, not a constant, (6)

because the prefix posterior q(u | y) depends on the candidate y. The discrepancy does not cancel in (5); the resulting guidance targets a different (prefix-averaged) distribution. Intuitively: whether a 4-token fragment is "rerouting around a removed edge" is undecidable without the route that led into it. The discriminator input must be the full partial path.

3. First-hitting instantiation (what is implemented)

Within the current block, the guided reveal follows the companion note's Eqs. (3)–(5) with the conditional weight of §2. At each first-hitting step (one forward pass, one token revealed):

  • Proposals. N = 100 candidates x₀b,(n) are drawn from the mean-field product of the SUBS marginals, x̂₀ = ∏ℓ∈masked pθ(x₀ℓ | xbt, x<b); revealed positions are copied through (frozen). An optional proposal temperature τ (companion §6 remedy) flattens the candidate distribution: x₀ ∼ softmax(logits/τ).
  • Weights. Each candidate is truncated at its first END (exclusive) / dst (inclusive) — partial paths never contain special tokens — padded with the dedicated PAD, and scored: w(n) = exp(γ · logitφ([x<b ∥ x₀b,(n)])), where exp(logit) = D/(1−D) and γ ≤ 1 is an optional tempering exponent. Row-max subtraction before exponentiation (shift-invariance of (5)) gives numerical stability.
  • Reveal target. x̄₀ℓ[k] ∝ Σn w(n) 𝟙{x₀(n),ℓ = k}; one uniformly-chosen masked position ℓ* is revealed by sampling from x̄₀ℓ* (absorbing posterior, companion Eq. 5). Zero-mass fallback: x̂₀.
  • Diagnostics. Per-step effective sample size ESS = (Σnw(n))²/Σn(w(n))² is logged. ESS ≈ N ⇔ flat weights ⇔ guidance inert (either clone candidates — the proposal-entropy bottleneck of companion §6, observed ESS ≈ 9.93/10 in the smoke run — or an uninformative discriminator). ESS ≈ 1 ⇔ weight collapse (variance).

3.1 Sequential composition and its bias

Across blocks the procedure is sequential self-normalized importance sampling without resampling: block b's guided kernel treats the guided prefix as exact. Two approximations stack per block: (i) the self-normalization bias of (5), O(1/N) for bounded weights (bounded by e2λγ under the λ-bounded logit head); (ii) the mean-field proposal p̂ replacing the true block posterior — identical in kind to the companion note's per-step approximation, not BD-specific. No further BD-specific approximation is introduced; in the B → canvas-length limit the scheme reduces to the fixed-canvas method, and at B = 1 to discriminator-reweighted autoregressive decoding.

5. Stabilizing the importance weights

  • Bounded logit head z ↦ λ·tanh(z/λ) (λ = 4): |logit| ≤ λ structurally caps the weight ratio at e2λ; the differentiable version of the clip-at-3 experiment in the prior project's logs.
  • Label smoothing ε = 0.05 in the BCE: keeps D off {0,1}, bounding the Bayes-target odds and preventing saturation (see the caveat of §2.1).
  • Negative-class coverage (the 'mix' ablation): addresses the out-of-support pathology at its source.
  • Weight tempering w ↦ wγ, γ ≤ 1: continuous variance–bias knob (γ = 0 recovers unguided sampling).
  • Self-normalization + row-max shift: exact shift-invariance used for numerics; Lemma 1 guarantees the shift is harmless.
  • ESS logging at every reveal: separates "guidance inert" (ESS ≈ N) from "guidance unstable" (ESS ≈ 1) — the two failure modes have opposite remedies (raise proposal entropy vs. temper weights).

6. Adjacency-masked proposals — Lemma 3 (exactness under the ideal discriminator)

Motivation (measured). The first experimental round found ESS ≈ 94–99 out of N = 100 at every block size: mean-field candidates are near-clones, so reweighting has nothing to choose between (the companion note's §6 bottleneck). Raising the proposal temperature adds isotropic entropy — mass spreads over all 1,390 vertices, almost none of it on legal reroutes — and degrades arrival faster than it helps (measured: τ = 2 costs −4 to −17%p arrival for −2 to −6%p removed-edge usage, ESS still ≈ 97). The fix is directed diversity: constrain the proposal to scenario-legal transitions.

6.1 The masked proposal

At a reveal step let M be the masked positions of the current block, and for each ℓ ∈ M let pℓ(·) be the model's SUBS marginal (shared by all candidates — they condition on the same xt and prefix). Define the legality mask from the revealed neighbours only:

mℓ(k) = Ascn[left(ℓ), k]𝟙{left revealed, real vertex} · Ascn[k, right(ℓ)]𝟙{right revealed, real vertex},   mℓ(END) = mℓ(PAD) = 1, (8)

and the masked mean-field proposal g(y) = ∏ℓ∈M pℓ(yℓ) mℓ(yℓ) / Zℓ, with Zℓ = Σk pℓ(k) mℓ(k) (fallback to unmasked pℓ if Zℓ ≈ 0). Because m depends only on revealed tokens — never on candidate-internal values — Zℓ is identical across candidates.

6.2 Lemma 3

Claim. Let w(y) = q(y | x<b)/pθ(y | x<b) be the exact conditional weight of Eq. (3) and f any statistic (e.g. the reveal indicator). If the target q assigns zero probability to paths using removed edges — true by construction, exceptional data are paths on the exceptional graph — then

𝔼y∼p̂[w(y) f(y)] = 𝔼y∼g[w(y) f(y)] · ∏ℓZℓ, (9)

so the self-normalized estimator over g-candidates with unchanged weights w (no p̂/g correction) targets exactly the same guided reveal distribution as the p̂-proposal estimator.

Proof. Exact importance identity with proposal change: 𝔼p̂[w f] = 𝔼g[(p̂/g) w f] + 𝔼p̂[w f 𝟙{y ∉ supp g}]. On supp g, p̂(y)/g(y) = ∏ℓ Zℓ/mℓ(yℓ) = ∏ℓ Zℓ (all m = 1 there), a candidate-independent constant. Off supp g, some position uses a transition absent from Ascn against a revealed neighbour; any completion y is then a path using a removed (or never-existing) edge, hence q(y | x<b) = 0 and w(y) = 0 — the excluded term vanishes identically, not approximately. Substituting into the self-normalized ratio, ∏Zℓ cancels between numerator and denominator (as C did in Lemma 1). ∎

Reading. Under the ideal discriminator, adjacency masking discards only candidates whose weight is exactly zero: it is a Rao-Blackwellization of the same estimator — all N samples land in the region where the target lives, which is precisely a variance reduction, never a bias. With a learned D the difference between the two estimators is bounded by the (spurious) weight mass D/(1−D) that the discriminator assigns to illegal candidates — the masked estimator simply deletes discriminator error on the illegal region.

6.3 Scope and caveats

  • Revealed-neighbour masking only. If masking instead chained through candidate-internal values (sequential within-candidate sampling), Z would depend on the candidate and the p̂/g correction would no longer be constant — the cancellation is specific to the position-independent mask (8). Consequence: two adjacent masked positions can still be mutually illegal inside one candidate; the D-weights (and the next reveal's updated mask) handle the residue.
  • Legality of special tokens. END/PAD are always legal (ending is not a traversal); MASK is already excluded by SUBS. The dst prefix (position 0) is not a traversal and imposes no constraint.
  • Unguided control. Masking without the discriminator (uniform weights over g-candidates ⇔ revealing directly from the masked marginal) isolates how much improvement comes from the constraint itself vs. from reweighting among legal reroutes — included in the experiment grid as adj-only.
  • Zero-support fallback. If no token is legal under (8) (Zℓ ≈ 0, e.g. a dead-end vertex in Ascn), the position falls back to the unmasked marginal; such reveals are the analogue of P1's unfixable pairs (0 observed so far).
