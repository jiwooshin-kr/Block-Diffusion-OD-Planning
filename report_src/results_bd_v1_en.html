Setup — identical to RESULTS_BD_prev.pdf except for the swept block size. BD3-LM-style block diffusion (semi-AR across blocks, absorbing/[MASK] diffusion within each block, SUBS weighted CE, first-hitting sampling). Transformer denoiser: 6 layers, dim 256, adaLN conditioning (~6.8M params; embedding table varies negligibly with canvas length). Trained 1 epoch = 48.2k iters (bs 32, lr 5e-4) on porto_v3_normal (index v3-0.05, 1,390 vertices, 1.93M paths). Canvas per the project length spec [dst, ori, v1, …, v_L=dst, END…END | PAD…PAD]: the END tail inside the dst block is a training target, later PAD blocks are excluded from the loss. Conditioning: dst + ori as clean in-context prefix tokens plus the OD embedding through adaLN; condition dropout 0.1; CFG off (w = 1). Open-ended generation (length_mode=open): blocks are appended until dst (hit) or END (miss) — no length model.

Evaluation. 1,000 held-out OD pairs per run, open-ended, raw only. hit = the path reaches dst unaided (= arrival, since no post-processing exists in this report); valid∧hit = usable-path rate; EM / PC / DTW / len metrics as in the companion reports. Caveat: each run draws its own random 1,000 evaluation pairs (dataset order is shuffled unseeded at load), so cross-run differences of ~±1%p are within sampling noise.

3. Findings (v2)

  • Held-out validation: no memorization inflation. The truly-held-out v2 numbers match v1's (train-overlapping) numbers within noise — blk1: 0.997/0.934 vs 0.997/0.943; blk4: 0.958/0.811 vs 0.943/0.821. Sec. 1's conclusions were not a product of leakage.
  • mask: small blocks win, blk1 outright (hit 0.997, valid∧hit 0.934). blk2–8 plateau (0.77–0.82; blk2 takes a slight edge in v2), cliff from blk16 (0.625 → 0.373). hit degrades gently (−16%p), validity steeply (−50%p) — the §4.3 random-reveal-order mechanism.
  • graph (d = 2.0) is the exact mirror image. Its adjacency-constrained decode keeps validity at 0.97–1.00 at every size, but arrival collapses with block size (0.429 → 0.027). The best graph cell is blk1 (0.429) — still under half of mask blk1 (0.997), and its walks wander (len err 36.4, len corr 0.24, 0.94 s/path). graph blk4's 0.128 is consistent with the predecessor report's 0.099.
  • Horizon behaviour also splits by kernel: mask shows no arrival decay with length for blk1–16 (§2 left); graph decays with length at every size (§2 right) — constrained walks reach distant destinations ever more rarely.
  • Length calibration: mask len corr ≥ +0.97 for blk ≥ 2 (dst-truncation geometry, §4.1); graph poor (0.24–0.78).
  • The cross-kernel verdict matches §6.9: mask = decisive but sloppy (repairable by post-processing); graph = legal but lost (the unrepairable kind). Block size charges mask in validity and graph in arrival.

4. Failure anatomy — where the length calibration comes from, what a miss is, and why validity falls

The measurements in this section are post-hoc diagnostics run on the saved checkpoints with fresh 400-pair draws (no refinement anywhere); with n_miss = 21–49 per run, subgroup percentages carry ~±10%p sampling noise.

4.1 The high len corr is mostly dst-truncation geometry, not END prediction

Generation length is decided by where the dst token is emitted for hit paths (the path is truncated at dst) and by where END is emitted only for the ~5–12% of miss paths. Splitting the correlation by subgroup:

block sizehitlen corr (all)len corr (hit only)len corr (miss only)hit detour ratio gen/real
640.8770.9850.9910.9521.011
160.9270.9880.9880.9701.011
40.9630.9800.9900.7781.012

Hit paths reach dst with only a ~1.1% detour over the real (shortest) path, so their length is pinned by the OD pair's graph distance — the 0.99 correlation follows from geometry. This is why len corr survives at blk64 even as validity collapses: what large blocks break is local edge consistency, not the global "how far until dst" structure. END calibration is a real but secondary effect: it governs only the misses, where it is also good (miss-only corr 0.95–0.97 at large blocks; miss mean lengths match real means) — without it, misses would run to the canvas cap and drag the overall correlation down.

4.2 A miss is a near-miss: the model reaches the destination's doorstep

A miss = the model emitted END (declared termination) without ever emitting the exact dst vertex; in this all-raw report no post-processing touches it and it counts as an arrival failure. Measuring the graph hop distance from each miss path's final vertex to dst:

block sizen_missmedian hops to dstmean≤1 hop≤2 hops≤5 hops

Combining hit rate with the miss-endpoint distribution, ~99% of all blk64 paths end within 2 hops of dst. A miss is a token-level identification failure at the very last step ("emit END instead of the exact dst id"), not a navigation failure — consistent with the length spec training END immediately after dst inside the same block. This is also why refine_es (cut at the first dst-adjacent vertex, append dst) could recover +3%p arrival in the predecessor report: half the misses already end dst-adjacent.

4.3 Why validity falls at large blocks: sparse local skips, amplified by a path-level metric

  • Amplification, not collapse. Per-edge accuracy is still 96.4% at blk64; with ~23 edges per path, P(all edges valid) ≈ 0.964²³ ≈ 0.43 — matching the observed 0.50 valid rate. The path-level all-or-nothing metric turns a 3.6% edge error rate into a 50% path failure rate.
  • The errors live strictly inside blocks. Zero invalid edges at block boundaries (where the previous block is fully committed and the transition is effectively autoregressive). The cause is first-hitting's random within-block reveal order: a position revealed between two already-committed non-adjacent anchors faces a two-sided constrained-infilling conditional, combinatorially harder than the left-to-right case and less accurately learned — the error rate therefore grows with block size.
  • Skips are local. Invalid pairs are truly 2–3 hops apart (median 3): the model drops a bridge vertex, it does not teleport.

4.4 Implications — remediation outlook

  • The model is not broken at large block sizes: global structure (endpoint placement, length, near-shortest routing) is intact; the defects are sparse (3.6% of edges), local (2–3 hop skips), and precisely locatable (adjacency check). This failure profile favors post-processing over retraining.
  • Local splice (proposed): replace each invalid edge (u, v) with the shortest path u→v (median insertion: 2 vertices). Validity → 1.0 by construction, endpoints untouched so hit is unchanged — at blk64 valid∧hit would rise from 0.454 to ≈ 0.877 (= hit) at an average cost of ~+1.7 tokens per path. Composable with the existing endpoint refinements (refine_nx) for a full valid=1 ∧ arrival=1 guarantee.
  • Training-side fixes are second-order: capacity/epochs/clipped schedules can lower the edge error rate but any-order infilling remains intrinsically harder than AR (the reference paper's own likelihood-vs-block-size trend).
  • Sampler-side fixes change the sampling distribution and need empirical validation: revealing left-to-right within a block (or adjacency-constrained logit masking, present in the prior project and deliberately removed) breaks the equivalence between first-hitting-with-uniform-choice and the trained diffusion reverse process — a learned model's conditionals are not order-consistent, and the prefix-conditioned patterns L→R relies on are rarely seen in random-masking training of large blocks.

4.5 Note (untested): the END-tail's loss share grows linearly with block size — a loss-engineering lever

Under the project length spec, every END token in the dst block's tail is an independent training target (build_canvas: the tail is inside loss_mask). Since the tail length is uniform over block residues, its expected size is (B−1)/2 tokens — so the composition of the supervised set shifts with block size (mean path ≈ 24):

block sizepath tokens in lossEND tokens (mean)END share of supervision
4≈23≈1.5≈6%
16≈23≈7.5≈25%
64 (= MDLM)≈23≈31.5≈58%
  • Both faces of the coin. Credit: the abundant END supervision is what §4.1–4.2 measure as excellent END calibration (miss-only len corr 0.95+, misses ending at plausible lengths). Suspected cost: END is an easy target (after dst the context fully determines it), so at large B the majority of the gradient goes to easy tokens, diluting the supervision of path tokens — a candidate partial explanation for §1's validity decay with block size. Untested hypothesis; the block-size effect also has the independent random-reveal-order mechanism of §4.3, so attribution requires a dedicated ablation.
  • Possible modifications (engineering, none run): (i) constant-mass END reweighting — scale the tail's per-token weight by c/(tail length), making the END loss share block-size-invariant (c ≈ 1–2 tokens' worth); (ii) first-END-only — supervise the first END at full weight and exclude the rest like PAD (the "single EOS loss" reading of the spec); (iii) decayed tail — geometric down-weighting within the tail. All compose with the −(1/t) MDLM weighting unchanged.
  • Cautions if ever tested: down-weighting risks the END calibration that §4.2 credits (miss lengths, doorstep stopping), so validity and miss-END calibration must be evaluated jointly; and any change breaks comparability with all runs in this report — test at blk16/64 first, where the share (25–58%) leaves the most room.

6.4 Findings

  • The absorbing-kernel instantiation works mechanically. No recurrence of the previously observed weight-variance explosion (bounded logits + label smoothing + negative-class coverage); discriminator val. accuracy 0.71–0.88 on partial paths; guidance delivers a consistent +4–5%p validity and +3–5%p EM/PC at every block size with arrival unchanged.
  • But the effect is small, and the cause is now measured, not conjectured: ESS ≈ 94–99 out of 100. The importance weights are nearly flat because the 100 mean-field candidates are near-clones — the proposal-entropy bottleneck predicted by MASKED_GUIDANCE.pdf §6, observed here in BD at every block size. Removed-edge usage barely moves (0.66 → 0.65): reweighting can only choose among offered candidates, and candidates avoiding the removed edges are almost never offered.
  • Unseen-scenario generalization (exp. 2) is the clearest positive result: a discriminator that never saw except_0 guides as well as (slightly better than) one trained on it. The scenario enters through the adjacency-lookup features, not through memorized paths.
  • Raising proposal temperature trades away more than it buys: rem-path −2 to −6%p at the cost of arrival −4 to −17%p, with ESS still ≈ 97. Flattening the same proposal is not enough.
  • Implication. The reweighting machinery is validated; the binding constraint is the proposal. The natural next steps keep the discriminator and change the candidate generator: (i) adjacency-masked proposals under Ascn (companion remedy (ii)) — candidates that cannot use removed edges, with IW guidance selecting among legal reroutes; and/or (ii) scoring-side use of the discriminator with a P1-style splice under the scenario graph.

6.10 Ablating the forward kernel: GDP graph-CTMC vs. pure uniform-state (D3PM)

The two forward kernels. GDP's kernel diffuses along the road graph: with generator G = A′ − D′ built from the (⟨end⟩-augmented) adjacency,

Qtgraph = exp((A′ − D′) βt) — local moves, slow global mixing (small spectral gap), graph structure retained in xt.

The classic pure uniform-state kernel is the complete-graph special case A′ = 𝟙𝟙T, which has the closed form

Qtunif = e−Kβt I + (1 − e−Kβt) 𝟙𝟙T/K  ⇔  D3PM-uniform with ᾱt = e−K Σs≤tβs (K = V+1 states),

i.e. survive-or-teleport-uniformly — no graph structure anywhere. Implemented by passing the complete graph to the same Destroyer; β is scaled by 1/K (7×10⁻⁸ … 7.2×10⁻³) so the per-step survival profile matches the graph kernel (the complete graph's spectral gap is K, so the unscaled schedule would fully mix by t = 2); the connectivity loss is off and the built-in constrained decode degenerates to unconstrained (complete graph) — the "pure" setting by construction. Same backbone, data, 1-epoch budget (48.9 min), D3PM ELBO, blk64 (= full canvas), and §6.8 protocol.
